Fettuccine Alfredo

Fettuccine Alfredo is a rich and classic pasta dish made with fettuccine noodles in a creamy sauce of butter, Parmeasan cheese, and often heavy cream.

Originating in Rome, the dish has a simple, traditional version of just pasta, butter, and cheese, while the Americanized version is a recher, cream-based sauce that can include additions like chicken, broccoli, or shrimp.

Ingredients:

  • 24 ounces dry fettuccine pasta
  • 1 cup butter
  • 3/4 pint heavy cream
  • Salt and pepper to taste
  • 1 dash garlic salt
  • 3/4 cup grated Romano cheese
  • 1/2 cup grated Parmesan cheese

Steps

  1. Gather all ingredients.
  2. Bring a large pot of lightly salted water to a boil. Add fettuccine pasta and cook for 8 to 10 minutes or until al dente; drain.
  3. Melt butter into cream in a large saucepan over low heat; add salt, pepper, and garlic salt.
  4. Increase the heat to medium; stir in grated Romano and Parmesan cheese until melted and sauce has thickened.
  5. Add cooked pasta to sauce and toss until thoroughly coated; serve immediately.
